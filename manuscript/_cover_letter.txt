Cover Letter

Dear Editor,

We submit our manuscript entitled "Multi-omics integration reveals Ogt downregulation drives RELA/NF-kB-mediated Nrf2 activation and SPP1-CD44 paracrine ferroptosis signaling in acute myocardial ischemia-reperfusion injury" for consideration at Cell Reports.

This study presents an integrative multi-omics characterization of the O-GlcNAc-Nrf2-ferroptosis regulatory axis in acute cardiac ischemia-reperfusion (I/R) injury. By combining two bulk RNA-seq cohorts (6,556 DEGs), single-nucleus transcriptomics (48,633 nuclei, 9 cell types), three independent proteomics platforms (LFQ and TMT), and cross-species external validation (GTEx v8, n = 431 healthy hearts; GSE57338, 177 HF vs 136 NF), we identify a cell-type-resolved signaling cascade: Ogt downregulation (log2FC = -0.56) in cardiomyocytes triggers RELA/NF-kB-mediated Nrf2 activation in myeloid cells, which is amplified by SPP1-CD44 paracrine crosstalk and executed through ferroptosis effector programs. Critically, we demonstrate that this signature is specific to acute I/R and is silenced or reversed in chronic human heart failure, establishing disease-stage specificity as a key dimension of Nrf2 biology in the heart.

We believe this manuscript is well-suited for Cell Reports for three reasons. First, it provides a multi-omics resource spanning transcriptomic, proteomic, single-nucleus, and intercellular signaling layers — a resource that will enable future mechanistic studies of O-GlcNAc signaling and ferroptosis in cardiac injury. Second, every major finding is validated by an orthogonal method or independent dataset: SPP1-CD44 paracrine signaling is confirmed by both CellChat and CellPhoneDB; Nrf2 pathway activation is corroborated by GSEA, ChEA upstream regulator prediction, and AUCell single-cell regulon scoring; and mRNA-protein discordance (~50% concordance) is replicated across three independent proteomics platforms (PXD046631, PXD040135, PXD035154) spanning LFQ and TMT quantification. Third, the cross-species validation (mouse, rat, human) and the honest reporting of disease-stage specificity (the Nrf2 signature is not a generic heart failure marker but an acute injury response) elevate the study beyond a single-cohort discovery exercise.

The novelty of this work lies in five key findings: (1) OGT does not directly O-GlcNAcylate the Nrf2/ferroptosis core machinery — RELA/NF-kB is the sole signaling intermediary connecting Ogt to Nrf2 activation; (2) the cell-type dichotomy (Ogt in cardiomyocytes/pericytes; Nrf2 effectors in myeloid cells) reveals paracrine rather than cell-autonomous regulation; (3) SPP1-CD44 is identified as the dominant myeloid-to-parenchymal signal by dual-method consensus; (4) ferritinophagy (FTH1 protein suppression despite mRNA induction) provides protein-level evidence for ferroptosis execution; and (5) the acute I/R specificity — confirmed by GTEx healthy baseline (OGT 39.2 vs HMOX1 3.0 TPM) and GSE57338 chronic HF reversal (31.5% directional concordance, binomial P = 1.0) — distinguishes this from a generic cardiac stress response.

All raw and processed data are publicly available at GEO (GSE193997, GSE307385, GSE240848, GSE163129, GSE57338) and ProteomeXchange/PRIDE (PXD035154, PXD040135, PXD046631). GTEx v8 data were accessed via the GTEx Portal. All analysis code has been deposited at GitHub ([URL to be inserted]) and is publicly available as of the date of publication.
