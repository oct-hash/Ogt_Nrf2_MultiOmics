三、SUMMARY / ABSTRACT (≤150 words)

Myocardial ischemia-reperfusion (I/R) injury triggers profound transcriptional
reprogramming, yet the molecular connections linking metabolic sensing to
antioxidant defense and cell death remain poorly defined. Here, we integrate
two bulk RNA-seq cohorts (6,556 DEGs), single-nucleus transcriptomics (48,633
nuclei, 9 cell types), three independent proteomics platforms, and cross-species
external validation to dissect the OGT-Nrf2-ferroptosis regulatory axis in acute
cardiac I/R. We identify Ogt downregulation (log2FC=-0.56) as a trigger for
Nrf2 pathway activation through RELA/NF-kB, the sole signaling intermediary
connecting OGT to Nrf2 — core Nrf2/ferroptosis proteins are absent from the OGT
interactome. Single-nucleus analysis reveals a cell-type dichotomy: Ogt in
cardiomyocytes and pericytes, Nrf2 effectors in myeloid cells, linked by
SPP1-CD44 paracrine signaling confirmed by dual-method consensus. Proteomics
demonstrates FTH1 suppression consistent with ferritinophagy. Critically, this
regulatory axis is specific to acute I/R and is silenced or reversed in chronic
human heart failure (GSE57338, 31.5% directional concordance), establishing
disease-stage specificity as a key dimension of Nrf2 biology in the heart.
