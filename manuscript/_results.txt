Multi-omics profiling identifies Ogt downregulation and coordinated Nrf2/ferroptosis activation in acute cardiac ischemia-reperfusion
Bulk RNA-seq discovery cohort
To characterize transcriptomic alterations in acute myocardial I/R injury, we performed bulk RNA-seq on left ventricular tissue from mice subjected to 1 h ischemia followed by 6 h reperfusion (I/R 6h) versus sham-operated controls. Differential expression analysis identified 6,556 genes with significant changes (adjusted P < 0.05; Fig 1B). Among these, O-linked N-acetylglucosamine transferase (Ogt), the sole enzyme catalyzing O-GlcNAcylation, was significantly downregulated (log2FC = -0.56, padj = 3.09 x 10^-7). In contrast, classical Nrf2 target genes were strongly induced: Hmox1 (log2FC = +4.43), Slc7a11 (log2FC = +4.07), and Gclc (log2FC = +0.84) were among the most highly upregulated genes in the dataset (Fig 1B, Fig 2B).
GSEA reveals pathway-level co-activation
Gene set enrichment analysis of the ranked DEG list confirmed a striking co-enrichment of the Nrf2-ARE pathway and the ferroptosis pathway. A combined Nrf2-ARE/ferroptosis custom gene set (47 curated genes, 43 detected) yielded NES = 1.77 (FDR = 0.001; Fig 2A). The FerrDb ferroptosis gene set was also significantly enriched (FDR < 0.05), confirming that both antioxidant and iron-dependent cell death programs are transcriptionally co-activated in the injured myocardium. These two pathways showed coordinated upregulation of their core components (Fig 2A-B), suggesting a functional link between Nrf2-mediated antioxidant response and ferroptosis regulation in the injured myocardium.
Healthy baseline establishes a switch-like pattern
To contextualize these disease-associated expression changes, we examined the baseline expression of core genes in healthy human left ventricle using the GTEx v8 resource (n = 431 donors). In stark contrast to the I/R state, Nrf2 target genes were virtually undetectable in healthy myocardium: Hmox1 median 3.0 TPM, Slc7a11 approximately 0 TPM. Conversely, OGT was constitutively expressed at moderate levels (39.2 TPM; Fig 2C). This 'off-in-health, on-in-disease' pattern suggests that the OGT-Nrf2-ferroptosis axis is specifically engaged during acute cardiac injury rather than functioning as a homeostatic program (Fig 2C).
Cross-platform proteomics reveals predominant post-transcriptional regulation and ferroptosis protein-level signatures
Given the moderate genome-wide correlation between mRNA and protein abundance reported across biological systems, we sought to determine whether the transcriptional signatures identified above were propagated to the proteome. We analyzed three independent proteomics datasets: PXD035154 (LFQ, two fractionated pools), PXD040135 (LFQ), and PXD046631 (LFQ). Across 2,308 genes with matched mRNA and protein quantification in PXD046631, we observed weak genome-wide correlation (R = 0.253, slope = 0.144; Fig 3). Directional concordance was 50.7%, indicating that approximately half of the protein changes mirrored their transcriptional counterparts, while the other half showed discordant regulation—consistent with substantial post-transcriptional and post-translational control (Fig 3).
Critically, several ferroptosis-related genes exhibited discordant mRNA-protein behavior. FTH1 (ferritin heavy chain) showed increased mRNA (log2FC = +0.36) but decreased protein (log2FC = -0.34, residual = -0.34), consistent with ferritinophagy-mediated ferritin degradation during ferroptosis. TFRC (transferrin receptor) mRNA decreased (log2FC = -1.19) while protein levels remained approximately unchanged (log2FC = +0.02, residual = +0.24), suggesting sustained iron import capacity despite transcriptional downregulation (Fig 3). Notably, Hmox1 and Slc7a11—the two most highly induced Nrf2 target genes at the mRNA level (log2FC = +4.43 and +4.07, respectively)—were below the mass spectrometry detection limit in all three proteomics datasets, indicating either translational delay or rapid protein degradation during the acute injury phase.
Single-nucleus transcriptomics uncovers a cell-type dichotomy: Ogt in cardiomyocytes, Nrf2 effectors in myeloid cells
To resolve the cell-type contributions underlying the bulk transcriptomic signatures, we analyzed a snRNA-seq dataset (GSE240848) comprising 48,633 cardiac nuclei annotated into 9 major cell types: Cardiomyocyte, Fibroblast, Endothelial, Macrophage, Neutrophil, Pericyte, Smooth Muscle Cell, T Cell, and B Cell (Fig 4A).
Strikingly, Ogt expression was predominantly localized to cardiomyocytes and pericytes, with minimal expression in immune cell populations (Fig 4B, 4D). In contrast, Nrf2 target genes Hmox1 and Slc7a11 showed a near-exclusive enrichment in myeloid cells, particularly macrophages and neutrophils (Fig 4C-D). This spatial dichotomy—Ogt in parenchymal/perivascular cells and Nrf2 effectors in infiltrating immune cells—suggested a paracrine signaling axis linking metabolic sensing in cardiomyocytes to inflammatory activation in myeloid cells. Quantitative analysis confirmed that Ogt was detected in 52.2% of cardiomyocyte nuclei and 47.0% of pericyte nuclei, but in only a minority of macrophage (<15%) and neutrophil (<5%) nuclei (Fig 4D). Conversely, Nfe2l2 was detected in 26.5% of macrophages, and its target Hmox1 in 20.2% of macrophages and 14.4% of neutrophils. Gpx4 showed low, uniform expression across all cell types (consistent with its modest bulk mRNA induction, log2FC = +0.25). Rela (NF-kB p65) was broadly expressed but most prominently in macrophages and neutrophils (Fig 4D). The Nrf2 pathway module score was selectively elevated in neutrophils and macrophages, whereas the O-GlcNAc pathway module score was highest in pericytes and cardiomyocytes, confirming the pathway-level segregation of these two transcriptional programs across cardiac cell types.
Dual-method cell-cell communication inference confirms SPP1-CD44 as a myeloid-derived paracrine ferroptosis amplifier
To test whether the observed cell-type dichotomy reflected active intercellular signaling, we performed cell-cell communication (CCC) inference using two independent algorithms: CellChat (mass-action model) and CellPhoneDB (permutation-based). From 157 myeloid-to-target interactions identified by CellChat and 528 by CellPhoneDB, we defined a high-confidence consensus set of 8 ligand-receptor pairs confirmed by both methods (Fig 5).
The dominant consensus signal was SPP1-CD44, detected from both macrophages and neutrophils to both cardiomyocytes and pericytes (CellChat score = 0.524-0.535, CellPhoneDB P < 0.001). SPP1 (osteopontin) is a pleiotropic cytokine secreted by activated macrophages, and CD44 is a cell-surface receptor known to be a direct Nrf2 transcriptional target. The SPP1-CD44 axis has been implicated in iron endocytosis and ferroptosis sensitivity. Additional consensus interactions included IGF1-IGF1R (growth factor signaling) and JAG1-NOTCH1 (morphogen signaling), primarily from macrophages to pericytes (Fig 5).
OGT substrate network perturbation reveals RELA/NF-kB as the signaling intermediary linking Ogt to Nrf2 activation
To investigate whether Ogt directly regulates the Nrf2/ferroptosis machinery through substrate O-GlcNAcylation, we queried the OGT-PIN v2.0 database of 782 high-stringency human OGT protein interaction partners against our 6,556 DEGs. OGT interactors were significantly enriched among DEGs (381/6,556, 5.81%; Fisher's exact test, OR = 2.01, P < 0.001), indicating broad perturbation of the OGT substrate network in I/R (Fig 6).
However, systematic examination of the Nrf2 pathway and ferroptosis core components revealed a striking pattern: none of the canonical Nrf2-ferroptosis proteins (NFE2L2, KEAP1, BACH1, HMOX1, SLC7A11, GPX4, FTH1, TFRC) were present in the OGT interactome (Fig 6). This absence suggests that OGT does not directly O-GlcNAcylate the Nrf2/ferroptosis machinery. Instead, RELA (NF-kB p65)—a known OGT interaction partner and substrate of O-GlcNAcylation—was both present in OGT-PIN and significantly upregulated at the mRNA level (log2FC = +1.13, padj = 5.3 x 10^-15). RELA was the sole transcription factor at the intersection of the OGT interactome and the I/R transcriptomic response.
These data support an indirect regulatory model in which Ogt downregulation alters O-GlcNAcylation of RELA/NF-kB, which in turn activates Nrf2 transcription. The Nrf2 targets (Hmox1, Slc7a11) then execute the ferroptosis resistance or iron-handling program, while myeloid-derived SPP1 signaling through CD44 amplifies the response (Fig 7C).
External validation confirms acute I/R specificity: Nrf2 targets are silenced in chronic human heart failure
To determine whether the OGT-Nrf2-ferroptosis activation signature generalizes beyond acute I/R, we performed cross-platform validation using a large human chronic heart failure cohort (GSE57338; 177 HF vs 136 NF, Affymetrix microarray). Among 5,514 genes with cross-species (mouse-to-human) and cross-platform (RNA-seq to microarray) ortholog mapping, directional concordance was 31.5% (1,640/5,211 with non-zero fold change), significantly below the 50% null expectation (binomial P = 1.0, right-tailed; Fig 7A).
Critically, the Nrf2 target genes that defined the acute I/R signature showed reversed regulation in chronic HF: Hmox1 (log2FC = +4.43 in acute mouse I/R vs -0.38 in chronic human HF) and Slc7a11 (+4.07 vs -0.30) moved in opposite directions (Fig 7A). Only the canonical Nrf2 target Nqo1 maintained consistent direction (-0.56 vs -0.53). Nfe2l2 and Gclc showed modest but concordant changes. These results indicate that the Nrf2/ferroptosis activation observed in acute I/R is not a generic feature of cardiac dysfunction but rather represents an injury-phase-specific response that is attenuated or reversed during chronic remodeling (Fig 7B).
